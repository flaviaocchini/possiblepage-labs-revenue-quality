PossiblePage Labs — Revenue Quality & Churn Risk Dashboard

Synthetic AI + business strategy case for a B2B no-code SaaS. The question is not only how much MRR Sales closed, but how much of that MRR remains healthy after 90/180 days.

1. Business recommendation

Add a Revenue Quality KPI to Sales incentives. Variable compensation should include a 90/180-day quality component: early churn, support ticket load, onboarding completion and customer health.

2. Why it matters

Low-fit and beta-promised customers can create revenue that looks good at close, but later generates tickets, CS overload, lower customer health and early churn.

This is a cross-functional problem: Sales, CS, Support and Product need aligned incentives.

3. Evidence stance

The dashboard shows strong directional evidence from synthetic data. The support-load spillover section is a proxy, not a causal proof.

Core management question

Closed revenue is not enough.

Sales can book MRR that remains >0 in CRM even if the account later churns. In this case, `closed_mrr_eur` means booked MRR at close, not active current MRR.

Support-load business impact

Toxic accounts generate 2.5× the first-90-day ticket volume of Healthy accounts.

High-ticket-volume months show lower overall customer sentiment in this synthetic dataset:

  • Avg tickets: 34.3 in lower-load months vs 318.0 in higher-load months.
  • Avg NPS: 27.6 vs 15.3.
  • Avg CSAT: 3.68 vs 3.38.
Interpretation: directional spillover proxy, not causal proof. The data supports the hypothesis that fragile revenue increases operational load and can deteriorate the broader customer experience.

Cleaning decision

`discount_pct` is recalculated from economic values: 1 - contract_value / gross_contract_value.

46 anomalies were documented; 4 impossible values were capped and flagged.

Operating model implication

When Sales closes low-quality revenue, the cost is paid by Support and Customer Success. Over time this can create friction between teams unless incentives are shared.
